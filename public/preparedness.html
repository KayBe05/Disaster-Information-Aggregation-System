Earthquake Safety Guide

Earthquakes strike without warning. Follow these steps to protect yourself and your family.

PREPARE What to do now
  • Secure heavy furniture to walls using brackets or straps
  • Store breakables on low shelves or in closed cabinets
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables, against interior walls)
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" with your family
  • Know how to turn off gas, water, and electricity
  • Keep shoes and flashlight by your bed
  • Retrofit your home if it's an older building (consult a structural engineer)
SURVIVE During the earthquake
DO NOT run outside during shaking! You are safer indoors under cover.
  • DROP to your hands and knees immediately
  • COVER your head and neck under a sturdy table or desk
  • HOLD ON to your shelter until shaking stops
  • If no table is nearby, crouch against an interior wall, cover your head
  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, hanging objects, and exterior walls
  • If in bed, stay there and cover your head with a pillow
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, power lines, and streetlights
  • If driving, pull over safely, stop, and stay inside until shaking ends
RECOVER After the shaking stops
  • Expect aftershocks â€“ be ready to Drop, Cover, Hold On again
  • Check yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid if trained
  • Check for hazards: gas leaks, damaged electrical wiring, structural damage
  • If you smell gas, open windows, leave immediately, and report it
  • Do NOT use elevators â€“ they may be damaged
  • Use text messages instead of calls to reduce network congestion
  • Inspect your home for cracks in foundation, chimney, or walls
  • Document damage with photos for insurance claims
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for official instructions
  • Stay out of damaged buildings until cleared by authorities

Flood Safety Guide

Floods are the most common natural disaster. Know the difference between a watch and a warning.

PREPARE What to do now
  • Know your flood risk â€“ check FEMA flood maps for your area
  • Purchase flood insurance â€“ standard homeowner's insurance doesn't cover floods
  • Create an evacuation plan with multiple routes to higher ground
  • Elevate utilities (furnace, water heater, electrical panel) above potential flood levels
  • Install check valves in plumbing to prevent backflow
  • Keep important documents in waterproof containers
  • Assemble emergency supplies in a waterproof container
SURVIVE During flooding
DO NOT enter floodwater! Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down. 2 feet can sweep away a vehicle.
  • Evacuate immediately if told to do so by authorities
  • Move to higher ground if water is rising
  • Never drive through flooded roads â€“ "Turn Around, Don't Drown"
  • Avoid walking through water â€“ it may hide debris, open manholes, or downed power lines
  • Stay off bridges over fast-moving water
  • If trapped in a building, go to the highest level (not the attic â€“ you could get trapped)
  • If trapped in a car, abandon it and move to higher ground immediately
  • Do NOT touch electrical equipment if wet or standing in water
RECOVER After the flood
  • Listen to authorities before returning home
  • Avoid floodwater â€“ it may be contaminated with sewage or chemicals
  • Do NOT enter buildings if water remains around them
  • Watch for structural damage â€“ walls, floors, and foundations may be compromised
  • Be aware of wildlife â€“ snakes and rodents may have entered your home
  • Throw away contaminated food that came in contact with floodwater
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
  • Document all damage for insurance with photos and videos
  • Boil water or use bottled water until authorities say tap water is safe

Wildfire Safety Guide

Wildfires spread rapidly. Create defensible space and be ready to evacuate at a moment's notice.

PREPARE What to do now
  • Create defensible space â€“ clear vegetation 30 feet around your home
  • Use fire-resistant materials for roofing and exterior walls
  • Install spark arresters on chimneys and stovepipes
  • Keep gutters and roof clear of leaves and debris
  • Store firewood 30 feet away from structures
  • Have garden hoses long enough to reach all areas of your home
  • Create an evacuation plan with two routes out of your neighborhood
  • Prepare a "go-bag" with essentials ready to grab in 5 minutes
SURVIVE During a wildfire
Evacuate immediately when ordered! Do not wait to see the fire. Fires can change direction rapidly.
  • Monitor emergency alerts and air quality reports
  • If advised to evacuate, do so immediately â€“ don't delay to gather belongings
  • If trapped, call 911 and give your location
  • Close all windows and doors but leave them unlocked
  • Turn on lights to help firefighters see your home through smoke
  • Fill sinks and tubs with water for firefighting
  • Move flammable items away from windows and doors
  • If caught in the open, go to a cleared area or lie flat and cover yourself with soil
  • Wear protective clothing â€“ cotton or wool, not synthetics
RECOVER After the fire
  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe
  • Watch for hot spots â€“ fires can reignite from hidden embers
  • Avoid damaged areas with weakened structures, trees, or power lines
  • Document property damage with photos for insurance
  • Discard contaminated food exposed to heat, smoke, or soot
  • Wear protective gear when cleaning ash â€“ use gloves, goggles, and N95 masks
  • Wet down ash before sweeping to minimize airborne particles
  • Check with utilities before turning on gas or electricity
  • Watch for respiratory issues from smoke inhalation â€“ seek medical help if needed

Hurricane Safety Guide

Hurricanes are tracked days in advance. Use that time to prepare and evacuate if necessary.

PREPARE What to do now
  • Know your evacuation zone â€“ coastal areas have designated zones
  • Strengthen your home â€“ install storm shutters or have plywood ready
  • Trim trees and shrubs to reduce wind damage
  • Clear gutters and drains to prevent water damage
  • Secure outdoor items â€“ bring in furniture, grills, and decorations
  • Review insurance â€“ ensure you have adequate coverage for wind and flood
  • Fill prescriptions in advance
  • Stock up on supplies â€“ water, non-perishable food, batteries, cash
SURVIVE During the hurricane
Evacuate if ordered! Storm surge can be deadly. Do not ride out a hurricane in an evacuation zone.
  • Monitor weather updates constantly via radio or mobile alerts
  • Charge all devices before losing power
  • Turn refrigerator to coldest setting to preserve food during outages
  • Fill bathtubs with water for sanitation
  • Stay indoors away from windows and glass doors
  • Go to an interior room on the lowest floor (not a basement)
  • Lie under sturdy furniture if winds become extreme
  • Beware the eye of the storm â€“ calm conditions are temporary, the second half is coming
  • Do NOT go outside until authorities declare the all-clear
RECOVER After the storm passes
  • Wait for official all-clear before leaving your shelter
  • Watch for flooding â€“ storm surge can continue after the hurricane
  • Avoid downed power lines and standing water near them
  • Check for structural damage before entering buildings
  • Use generators outdoors only â€“ never inside due to carbon monoxide risk
  • Do NOT use candles if gas leaks are suspected
  • Conserve water until water systems are declared safe
  • Report downed power lines to utility companies
  • Help neighbors but do not enter damaged buildings
  • Document damage thoroughly for FEMA and insurance claims
